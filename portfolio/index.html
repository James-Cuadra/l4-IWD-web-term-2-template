The Benefits of Cucumbers

With an interest in food and nutrition since a young age, I have always been fascinated with the varying opinions surrounding "superfoods" and their supposed benefits when included in a diet. As I got older, I naturally formed my own opinions around the matter and settled on the following;

the real superfood is the Cucumber

I realise this may come across as a controversial statement for the majority of readers, so let me explain my reasoning.

The cucumber (Cucumis sativus) is a creeping vine plant belonging to the gourd family, a family including sqaushes and melons. The cucumber's fruit is widely believed to be a vegetable, however this is far from the truth. The cucumber is classified as a modified berry, with a thick outer rind and no internal divisions, similar to a melon.

The cucumber is predominantly a carbohydrate, but with 95% of it's mass comprised of water it is lower in calorie than most other carbs. The other 5% of it's mass is mainly the rind, containing natural sugars and trace amounts of protein. However, the beauty of the cucumber comes not from it's macronutrients, but its micronutrients.

According to Healthline 300g serving of cucumber contains the following micronutrients;

Nutrient % of RDI
Fiber 7
Vitamin C 14
Vitamin K 62
